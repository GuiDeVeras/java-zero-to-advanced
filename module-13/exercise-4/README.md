Exercise 4
	Write a program to read the data of N taxpayers (N provided by the user), who can be either an individual (natural person) or a company (legal entity). Then, show the amount of tax paid by each one, as well as the total tax collected.

	The data for an individual are: name, annual income, and health expenditures. The data for a company are: name, annual income, and number of employees. The rules for calculating the tax are as follows:

	Individual: people whose income was below 20000.00 pay 15% tax. People with an income of 20000.00 and above pay 25% tax. If the person had health expenditures, 50% of these expenditures are deducted from the tax.

	    Example: a person whose income was 50000.00 and had 20000.00 in health expenditures, the tax is: (50000 * 25%) - (2000 * 50%) = 11500.00

	Company: companies pay 16% tax. However, if the company has more than 10 employees, it pays 14% tax.

	    Example: a company whose income was 400000.00 and has 25 employees, the tax is: 400000 * 14% = 56000.00"
